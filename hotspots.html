Shark Hotspots & Behavior

Global Shark Hotspots

Sharks gather in certain parts of the oceans for feeding, breeding, and migration. Famous hotspots include the Great Barrier Reef, South Africa’s coast, and Guadalupe Island. These areas are rich in prey and safe for reproduction, making them essential for shark survival.

Where sharks love to be!

Tracking Shark Movement

Scientists attach satellite tags to sharks to track their movements. These tags reveal migration paths, hunting zones, and seasonal behavior patterns. Tracking helps researchers understand sharks’ routines, protect them, and study how they respond to changes in the ocean.

Technology meets ocean life.

Ocean Conditions & Shark Habitats

Sharks select habitats based on water temperature, currents, and food availability. Areas with high chlorophyll attract prey, while warm waters provide safe spots for breeding. Ocean conditions directly influence where sharks live and when they migrate.

Sharks follow nature’s rules.
