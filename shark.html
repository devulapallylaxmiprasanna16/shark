Great White Shark

Up to 6 m | Diet: Seals, Fish | Habitat: Coastal Waters

Can detect one drop of blood in 25 gallons of water.

Hammerhead Shark

Up to 6 m | Diet: Rays, Squid | Habitat: Coastal & Open Ocean

Head shape helps detect prey more effectively.

Whale Shark

Up to 18 m | Diet: Plankton | Habitat: Warm Oceans

Largest fish in the ocean, gentle giant.

Tiger Shark

Up to 5 m | Diet: Fish, seals, turtles | Habitat: Tropical & temperate seas

Known as the "garbage eater" of the sea due to its varied diet.

Nurse Shark

Up to 3 m | Diet: Crustaceans, small fish | Habitat: Warm coastal waters

Often rests in groups during the day and hunts at night.

Mako Shark

Up to 4 m | Diet: Fish, squid | Habitat: Open ocean

Fastest shark in the ocean, can reach speeds up to 70 km/h.

Leopard Shark

Up to 1.5 m | Diet: Clams, worms, fish | Habitat: Shallow coastal waters

Named for its striking leopard-like spots on its body.

Thresher Shark

Up to 6 m | Diet: Small fish | Habitat: Coastal & open ocean

Uses its long tail to stun schools of fish before eating.

Bull Shark

Up to 3.5 m | Diet: Fish, dolphins, other sharks | Habitat: Rivers & coastal seas

Can live in both saltwater and freshwater environments.

Dusky Shark
