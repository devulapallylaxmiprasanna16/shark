About — Sharks from Space

Project Overview

About Sharks from Space Sharks from Space is a project created for NASA’s 2025 Space Apps Challenge. It uses NASA’s satellite data to track and study shark movements and habitats. The project analyzes ocean data, like currents and temperature, to predict where sharks forage, helping with marine conservation. Developed for a global NASA hackathon, it shows how space technology can protect ocean ecosystems. By using NASA’s data, Sharks from Space supports shark research and ocean biodiversity preservation.

A new era of marine exploration.

Project Objective

Develop a mathematical framework to identify sharks and predict their foraging habitats using NASA satellite data.Analyze the ecological links between oceanographic features, phytoplankton communities, and shark movements.Propose innovative tools, such as electronic tags, to track shark location and feeding behavior in real-time.Support marine conservation efforts by improving understanding of shark habitats and behavior from space-based data.

A new era of marine exploration.

Working Principle

Sharks from Space analyzes NASA satellite data on ocean conditions like currents, temperature, and biological activity. Using mathematical models, it identifies shark locations and predicts their feeding areas. This helps track shark movements and supports marine conservation through remote sensing technology.

Remote sensing meets marine science.

Summary

Sharks from Space uses NASA’s satellite data to create a mathematical framework for identifying sharks and predicting their feeding habitats. This project helps scientists track shark movements and supports marine conservation efforts by analyzing ocean conditions from space. It demonstrates how NASA’s Earth observation technology can be used to protect ocean biodiversity.

Protection through prediction.

Impact

Sharks from Space helps protect shark populations by providing critical data on their movements and habitats using NASA’s satellite technology. This improves scientific understanding and supports conservation efforts to preserve marine biodiversity. The project demonstrates how space data can solve real-world environmental challenges.

Turning data into ocean wisdom.
